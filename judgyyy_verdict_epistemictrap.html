What JUDGYYY flags penalties
Human baseline — the critical gap (−7 pts)
This is JUDGYYY's biggest deduction and the single thing that separates a track win from a grand prize. No human performance data is collected or reported. The writeup claims the CCC tasks are checkable but doesn't establish human accuracy curves. The Pressure "knowable" baseline is trivially 100% for educated adults — but this isn't stated. Plomecka and the cognitive framework paper require demographically representative human baselines. Without them, the benchmark cannot be placed in the 3-stage evaluation protocol DeepMind proposes.
11/18
CCC tasks — subtle construct validity issue (−2 pts)
The easy CCC items (arithmetic + mod operations) have a confound: the steps are nearly identical (step 1 = compute X, step 2 = same computation restated). A model can get calibration right by tripling confidence on trivial steps — but this doesn't test epistemic drift through genuinely dependent, uncertain reasoning. The hard items (binomial fractions) are better. The batch needs more probability and science domain chains where uncertainty genuinely compounds.
−2 pts
Pressure task — multi-turn architecture concern (−1 pt)
The 3 turns are stateless — each LLM call is independent (no conversation history passed). This means the model isn't actually experiencing social pressure in a multi-turn sense; it's three separate prompts. A model with perfect turn-1 accuracy but zero social-pressure sensitivity would score 1/6 per turn correctly, indistinguishable from one that genuinely resists. The design should pass prior turns as context.
−1 pt
